So who made JavaScript?

JavaScript was built in 10 days by Brendan Eich in May of 1995. Originally called Mocha.
A bit of history

In September of '95 the name changed to Livescript and in December it was offically called Javascript. From '96-'97 Javascript was taken to Ecma To create standard specifications, so that browsers could implement them.
What is Ecma?

Ecma is an institution for standardizing information and communication systems. Their aim is to standardize the use of information communication technologies and consumer electronics.
